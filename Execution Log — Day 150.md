**Session Type:** Core Session



**Topic:** Amdahl’s Law



**Concept:**

Amdahl’s Law states that the maximum performance improvement from parallelism is limited by the portion of a system that must remain sequential.



**Key Learnings:**

* Parallelism has fundamental scalability limits
* Sequential bottlenecks restrict maximum speedup
* Adding more cores/processors eventually gives diminishing returns
* Performance optimization requires identifying limiting factors



**Core Insight:**

System scalability is ultimately constrained by unavoidable sequential bottlenecks and coordination overhead.



**Outcome:**

* Strengthened understanding of parallel performance limits
* Connected bottlenecks with scalability engineering
* Improved reasoning about realistic optimization strategies
