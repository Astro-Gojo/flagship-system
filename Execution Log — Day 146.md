**Day 146 — Execution Log**  **| Date : 16/05/2026**



**Session Type:** Core Session



**Topic:** Atomic Operations



**Concept:**

Atomic operations are indivisible operations that complete fully without interruption, ensuring safe concurrent updates.



**Key Learnings:**

* Atomic operations prevent partial/interleaved updates
* Normal operations like increment involve multiple unsafe internal steps
* Modern CPUs provide hardware-supported atomic instructions
* Synchronization mechanisms rely on atomicity internally



**Core Insight:**

Atomicity forms the hardware-level foundation of safe synchronization in concurrent systems.



**Outcome:**

* Strengthened understanding of low-level synchronization foundations
* Connected race conditions with atomic update safety
* Improved reasoning about hardware-assisted concurrency control
