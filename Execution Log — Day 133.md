**Session Type:** Core Session



**Topic:** Concurrency vs Parallelism



**Concept:**

Concurrency is the management of multiple tasks, while parallelism is the simultaneous execution of tasks.



**Key Learnings:**

* Concurrency does not require multiple cores
* Parallelism requires multiple cores
* Systems can be concurrent without being parallel
* Most modern systems use both together



**Core Insight:**

Concurrency structures tasks, while parallelism executes them simultaneously.



**Outcome:**

* Strengthened understanding of task management vs execution
* Reinforced previous knowledge with deeper clarity
* Connected concepts with real system behavior
