**Session Type:** Core Session



**Topic:** Lock-Free Programming



**Concept:**

Lock-free programming uses atomic operations to coordinate concurrent access without relying on traditional blocking locks.



**Key Learnings:**

* Lock-free systems reduce blocking and contention
* Atomic operations are the foundation of lock-free synchronization
* Lock-free approaches can improve scalability and reduce overhead
* Designing lock-free systems is significantly more complex



**Core Insight:**

High-performance concurrency often reduces blocking by replacing traditional locks with atomic coordination techniques.



**Outcome:**

* Strengthened understanding of advanced synchronization strategies
* Connected atomic operations with scalable concurrency design
* Improved reasoning about performance vs complexity trade-offs
