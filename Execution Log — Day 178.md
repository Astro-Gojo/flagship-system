**Session Type:** Micro Session



**Topic:** Quorum



**Concepts Learned:**

* Quorum is the minimum number of machines required to make decisions.
* Majority agreement is preferred over unanimous agreement.
* Quorum allows systems to tolerate failures and continue operating.



**Key Insights:**

* Waiting for every machine can prevent progress.
* Distributed systems must handle delays and failures gracefully.
* Majority voting improves availability.



**Systems Engineering Principle:**

Reliable systems prioritize progress under failures rather than demanding perfect participation.



**Progression:**

Systems understanding expanded from:

* leader election

→ consensus

→ quorum

→ majority agreement
