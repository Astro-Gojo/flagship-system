**Day 114 — Execution Log | Date : 14/04/2026**





**Session Type:** Core Session



**Topic:** Deadlock Prevention Strategies



**Concept:**

Deadlock can be prevented by breaking at least one of its four necessary conditions.



**Strategies Learned:**

* Break hold-and-wait (request all resources at once or release before requesting)
* Allow preemption (force resource release)
* Resource ordering (fixed order of acquisition)
* Reduce mutual exclusion (share resources where possible)



**Key Learnings:**

* Resource ordering is the most practical and widely used strategy
* Deadlock prevention requires design constraints and trade-offs
* Systems should be designed to avoid deadlock rather than detect it



**Core Insight:**

Deadlock prevention is achieved through disciplined system design, not reactive fixes.



**Outcome:**

* Learned practical strategies to avoid deadlock
* Strengthened ability to design safe concurrent systems
* Improved system-level thinking
